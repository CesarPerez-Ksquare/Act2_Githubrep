History of San Cristóbal

San Cristóbal is one of the 32 provinces of the Dominican Republic and is the fourth most populated province in the country.

It was created in 1932 with the name "Provincia Trujillo" in honor of its founder, the dictator Rafael Leónidas Trujillo Molina. After Trujillo's death in 1961, it was given its current name of San Cristóbal.

The "Monumento a los Constituyentes", pride of the people of San Cristobal, was inaugurated on April 26, 2006.

The 33 vertical structures of the monument symbolize the 33 redactors of the first Constitution that was signed on November 6, 1844.
